Where to start, just like any journey the starting point is the hardest I believe. It's filled with alot of doubt, fear of whatever it is you're losing, grief for what was and some sort of feeling like a mixture of excitment and dread for what's ahead. I mean if you think about logically, the starting point is where you're "made" or "broken" and it makes sense, self preservation is a slow killer masked as self help and a leap of faith is madness dressed in the kings invisble garments. Tell me how risking safety and security for a promise that only you can see(in your minds eye) is worth anything? I spend so much of my time trying to convince myself that maybe that it'll make sense but that is never the case. That's how i feel about the beginning. It's confusing, you're fumbling every step you take and are ashamed of the fact that a person could be this clueless LOL. But then you realise ukuthi that's just the "starting point" what comes after couldn't possibly be worse than that right? Then what comes after is totally worse(LMAO), you're fumbling worse than you did at the beginning and worse you realise ukuthi you chose this, yup you saw the challenges at the starting point and where like "yeah, gimme more of that" but this time it hurts different. Not a physical hurt or emotional hurt but a different hurt, from somewhere inside you. Even looking at yourself in the mirror is a weird experience now. Partly because you can't recognise the person looking back but mostly because you are the main source of your pain. You're the one hurting and the one hurting yourself. Haha. But maybe that's the point, everything breaks, everything gets destroyed, worlds blow up, stars burn out, building falls, people die and versions of self that no longer serve also "need" to die. Don't get me wrong this may sound dreadful but if you really look at it, there's a beautiful symphony to it. I mean God Himself said "I bring good and i bring evil(paraphasing), I the LORD do these things" possibly meaning the two aren't so different from one another. Like how the starting point is possibly no different from the finish line, you just have to chose how you do inbetween the two. So "toast" the Journey, no one ever said it would be easy but Mom always said "Kuzolunga" and i"m starting to believe that. It doesn't get any better, its does get easier though, cause you got better. So with my metaphysical glass I toast to it getting worse cause that simply means I'm getting better. So yay.
